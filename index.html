Phase 1 Course Goals


1. Foster the creative habits of mind

Fostering creative habits of the mind is very important to the art of design. Coming up with ideas and reflecting and expanding upon them can make or break a design. In these past design challenges I try to come up with new ideas and go beyond. For example in the webtext assignments instead of solely focusing on the preset code given to us, I wanted to go beyond and use the knowledge I had to create something that the preset code wouldnt allow me to do. In workshops hearing feedback allows me to reflect upon my designs and incorporate the new ideas from my classmates into my work.

2. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

By engaging with others during workshops I was able gain insight/feedback from my classmates to expand on my designs. While I thought something looked fine or was good how it is, when speaking with others there were things that were pointed out that I didn't notice/ advice to improve on. I can workshop with a prototype design and leave with new ideas for a more refined piece of work. With the good design project, it was still a work in progress when I shared it during the workshop. My classmates gave me advice like putting an outline around the frame, making the boxes and sub title the same color, and moving the frames around which I incorporated into my final design. Also if I have questions or am struggling my classmates were able to help me through it.

3. Think deeply about craft.

I always want my work to be the best I can do in that moment. Thinking deeply about the craft and what I am making really helped me keep it in mind when designing. Throughout each assignment in phase one I thought about things that may seem trivial to make sure I was happy with what I am turning in. For example, what color should background be, what alignment should I use, or what photo to use etc.

4. Engage in sustained research and skills development.

Sometimes when designing a designer may not know everything or how to do things and that is where research can come to play. Or being lost on where to go with a design or what to do research can help with that too and develop skills at the same time. Through the webtext assignments when something was wrong with my code or something didnt work I would research and try to figure out what was wrong. I was able to fix the problem, but in the process learned a new skill. I also did research before starting the assignments because I never knew what to do or how to start.

5. Learn to present your work

Through these design projects, workshops, and discussions I was able to slowly figure out how to present my works to others. I presented my design to others in a way that truly expresses my work and presented it in a way I want not only my designs but myself to be seen as a designer through my creative work. For example, with all of my designs so far sharing them in the workshop I had to explain my designs, thought process, and explain why I did things the way I did because everyone thinks differently.
